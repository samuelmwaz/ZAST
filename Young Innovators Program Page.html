About the Program

Where Ideas Meet Execution

The ZAST Young Innovators Program (YIP) is a structured 12-week launchpad that takes young Zambians with big ideas and puts them in an environment where those ideas become real-world solutions. No fluff — just mentorship, tools, and accountability.

Selected innovators work within the ZAST ecosystem, gaining access to technical resources, experienced builders, and a network designed to fund and scale what works. Your ideas matter. Execution is everything.

Who Should Apply?

Young Zambians aged 18–30 with an idea worth building. Whether you're a student, a developer, a designer, or a first-time entrepreneur — if you're serious about building something real, this program is for you.

What Do You Need?

No prior startup experience required. You need an idea, the drive to execute, and the discipline to show up every week. We provide everything else — tools, mentors, structure, and platform.

Time Commitment

Expect to invest 15–20 hours per week over 12 weeks. This is intensive by design — the same commitment you'd give a job, directed at building your own future.

Eligibility

Are You the Right Fit?

We're looking for driven young Zambians who are ready to move from ideas to action. Here's what we look for in candidates.

Age 18 – 30
Open to young Zambians within this age range — students, graduates, and early-career professionals all welcome.
Zambian Citizen or Resident
This program is built for Zambia's future. Priority given to citizens and long-term residents committed to local impact.
A Real Idea to Build
You don't need a finished product — but you need a clear problem you want to solve and a vision for how to solve it.
Commitment to 12 Weeks
This isn't a casual programme. You must be ready to invest 15–20 hours per week for the full 12-week cohort.
Any Background Welcome
Tech, business, design, or none of the above — diverse skill sets make stronger teams. Prior startup experience is not required.
Local-Impact Mindset
We prioritise ideas that solve real problems in Zambia — from logistics and employment to health, finance, and beyond.
Innovation Tracks

Choose Your Build Track

Three focused tracks aligned to Zambia's highest-impact sectors. Your idea, your direction — we provide the structure to get it built.

Tech & Software
Build apps, platforms, and digital tools that solve real Zambian problems at scale.
  • Mobile and web applications
  • SaaS platforms and APIs
  • AI and data-driven tools
  • Fintech and digital wallets
Social & Community
Create solutions that directly improve livelihoods, education, health, or community infrastructure.
  • EdTech and learning tools
  • Health access platforms
  • Community organising systems
  • Agricultural solutions
Business & Operations
Formalise and scale informal businesses through smarter systems, logistics, and operational tools.
  • Logistics and supply chain
  • Employment and HR systems
  • E-commerce platforms
  • SME management tools
Program Timeline

The 12-Week Journey

Four phases, each building on the last. From raw concept to launched product — here's how we get there together.

01
Discovery
Weeks 1–2. Validate your idea. Define the problem, identify your users, and stress-test assumptions with real feedback.
02
Design & Plan
Weeks 3–4. Map your solution architecture, user journey, and MVP scope. Set the blueprint before you build.
03
Build & Iterate
Weeks 5–10. The intensive build phase. Ship your MVP, gather real user feedback, and iterate fast with mentor guidance.
04
Launch & Pitch
Weeks 11–12. Finalise your product, prepare your pitch, and present on Demo Day to investors and the ZAST community.
